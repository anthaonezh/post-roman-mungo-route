# Summary of Vita Sancti Kentigerni (Jocelin of Furness)

## Key Passages Relevant to Mungo's Movements

### Early Life and Training
- Kentigern (Mungo) was born of noble lineage, possibly a princess of Lothian.
- He was educated under St Serf at Culross (Fife).

### Journey to Glasgow
- After leaving Culross, Mungo travelled to the site of Glasgow, then known as "Cathures" or "Glasgu".
- The Vita describes his journey as guided by divine providence, but mentions crossing rivers and forests.
- He established a church at Glasgow, which later became the cathedral.

### Exile and Return
- Due to political turmoil, Mungo was forced into exile.
- He travelled to Wales, where he spent time in the kingdom of Glywysing (modern Glamorgan).
- In Wales, he founded a monastery at Llanelwy (St Asaph) and engaged with local Christian communities.
- After some years, he returned to Glasgow, restoring his episcopal see.

### Later Life
- He continued missionary work in the Clyde valley and beyond.
- His miracles and pastoral care are recorded, cementing his status as patron saint of Glasgow.

## Geographical References
- **Culross** (Fife, Scotland)
- **Glasgow** (Clyde valley, Strathclyde)
- **Glywysing** (south Wales)
- **Llanelwy** (St Asaph, north Wales)
- Mention of crossing the "Sea of Alcluith" (Firth of Clyde) and travelling through "wooded hills".

## Notes for Route Analysis
The Vita provides only general directions, not detailed itineraries. However, the mention of Wales and return suggests a maritime or overland route linking the Clyde with the Welsh coast. The text implies a journey of several weeks, indicating a substantial distance covered, likely using established tracks or waterways.
